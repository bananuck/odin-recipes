Mochi Taiyaki

Description

A chewier spin on the classic Japanese favourite! The texture is soft and chewy on the inside and nice and crispy on the outside.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 25 g of white sugar
  • 2 g of salt
  • 450 g of milk
  • 5 g of vanilla extract
  • 120 g of all-purpose flour
  • 130 g of glutinous rice flour (Mochiko)
  • 15 g of baking powder
  • Any filling you like, such as red bean paste, cheese, pudding, Nutella, etc.

Special equipment

  • Taiyaki waffle iron or pan

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, beat the eggs and sugar together until creamy in texture.
  2. Add salt, milk, and vanilla extract and mix well.
  3. Add the flour and baking powder and mix thoroughly. The batter should be slightly runnier than a pancake batter. If it is too thick, add a splash more milk.
  4. Heat up the taiyaki waffle iron or pan.
  5. Pour in just enough batter onto the bottom side to cover it.
  6. Add some filling in the centre of the taiyaki (do not over fill).
  7. Pour in more batter over the filling then close the waffle iron or pan.
  8. Cook each side for approximately 70-90 seconds. Time may vary due to different waffle irons or stoves. Check if ready by opening the iron or pan.
  9. Take out the cooked taiyaki and cool on a cooling rack.
  10. Repeat the above steps until the batter runs out.
